IBM's Personal Computer

on August 12, 1981, the IBM Personal computer would be introduced to the public as one of the most key milestones (in my opinion) in the development of a computer, logical device or software. A group of containing 12 strategists were picked, working around the clock to come up with a plan for hardware, software, manufacturing setup and how to sale the product. The development team went outside of the traditional boundaries of product development within IMB, the team would go to outside vendors to buy their parts such as a CPU and other products needed to compose the IBM personal computer, doing this, they were able to release the product in 12 months.
